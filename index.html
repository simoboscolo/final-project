to Rome, by bike

Never taught i was going to do it, but eventually when my friends asked me: "hey wanna come by bike to Rome this summer?" i answered "yeah, why not?". Well, Spoiler Alert: We did it. No preparation at all, 700 km, 12 days and a bit too much money later we were eating spaghetti cacio e pepe. Totally worth it.

DAY ONE

Orio Litta - 62km

CLOSE

Step 1: take the bike. Step 2: bike. It sounded easy and honestly the first day was not quite chill. Eventually we learned it's important to have water with you at all times because apparently it's impossible to get it when you're biking in the middle of nothing in the Italian Countryside.

We also learned you can't bike on sand so there was a lot of walking and Google Maps became our best friend. The sand was ending 'over there' every five minutes, only that it was actually not.

On top of it a little extra shortcut made our first day something like 10km longer. Lesson number 3 is do not shortcut.

We arrived at Orio Litta around Dinner time and, as we contacted the major during the day, we stayed at the town hall for the night. Lucky thing is, there was a small city fair, so we relaxed with a beer and funky live music in the evening.

DAY TWO

Sant'Andrea Terme - 100km

CLOSE

The Second day was fun! We started biking early in the morning and soon realized the Po Valley was over. We were going up!

At the first decent city we stop to have a look around and here we meet Susan. We met her at our first night, she was staying at the city hall as well. She was like us travelling. She was from New Zeland and alike us she had so much energy i don't even know how to decribe it! She explained us she started walking from Spain and then went up to North Cape and then came down to Italy and after reaching Rome she was going to go back up and then to Jerusalem. All Walking. She started 2 years before and as we met her in 2013 I guess she should be in the Holy Land about now?

She played for us a beatiful song with the flute and then she was off again walking. I still don't know where she was getting the energy from.

After lunch we started biking again going up and up, legs hurting. As a reward we decided to stop for the night in a small thermal village, in a nice resort. We had some good food, loads of it, enjoying music.

DAY THREE

Cassio - 30km

CLOSE

We had no idea what we were going into! The Po Valley was now really fucking over and we where dying climbing up mountains we didn't even know existed.

So basically i was the only one stubborn enough to keep biking, because every ten minutes i woudl turn my head and realized i was alone. So i would wait for other ten minutes under the burning sun of July and see the first of my friends coming. The second one was not even trying to bike so we would have to wait ten minutes more for him. After twenty minutes waiting then i would start bike. And the second friend would start shouting at me as he didn't have time to relax. From what i don't know because he was basically walkung carrying a bycicle but ok, let's wait ten minutes more.

And that's how we managed to do only 30km in one day.

DAY FOUR

Pontremoli - 45km

CLOSE

Woke up early knowing we still had 15 km to go till the end of the mountain. We stopped for a quick break in a small town on the way and then up again.

After we reached the top we started to go down! so fast! At some point we were going 60km per hour and the slowest friend got scared and started going slow again

At the first Town we stop ad decide was time to relax and have a look around, was very very nice.
